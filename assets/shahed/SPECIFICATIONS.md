## Altitude Profile

| Phase              | Altitude        | Notes                                    |
|--------------------|-----------------|------------------------------------------|
| Minimum flight     | 20 m            | Terrain-following along rivers/roads     |
| Low approach       | 60–200 m        | Target area, final approach              |
| Cruise (march)     | 700–2,000 m     | Typical en-route altitude                |
| Max ceiling        | 4,000 m         | Maximum operational ceiling              |

### Typical Attack Profile
1. **Launch** — rocket-assisted takeoff (RATO) from ground rack or vehicle
2. **Climb** — ascent to 700–2,000 m cruise altitude
3. **March** — GPS/INS navigation at 140–150 km/h, 700–2,000 m
4. **Descent** — drops to 200 m approaching target area
5. **Terminal** — final dive to target, can go as low as 20 m

## Navigation

| Parameter          | Value                                |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Primary            | INS + GPS                            |
| Radio update range | ~150 km (target location correction) |
| Swarm capable      | Yes — multiple units from one station|

## Radar Cross Section (RCS)
- Very low due to composite construction and small size
- Comparable to a large bird (~0.01–0.1 m²)
- Difficult to detect on conventional radar at low altitudes
